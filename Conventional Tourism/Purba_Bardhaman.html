Curzon Gate

The Curzon Gate is a prominent landmark in Bardhaman city in Purba Bardhaman district in West Bengal, India,Located at the crossing of Bijoy Chand Road and Grand Trunk Road, it was built in 1902/1903, on the occasion of the coronation of Maharaja Bijay Chand Mahatab as ruler of the Bardhaman Raj. The former royal palace is located 1 km from the gate

Sarbamangala Mandir

The idol of Mata Sarbamangala is very very old (approx. 1000 years old) from time immemorial, people felt the existence of it in old literatures and holy books. This is an idol of Mata Durga with eighteen hands and bestowed on Lion MAHISHA MARDINI and is the first NABARATNA temple in the undivided Bengal.

Tomb of Sher Afghan

His tomb is situated within the shrine complex of the poet Bahram Saqqa, in the present day city of Bardhaman in West Bengal, next to the tomb of Qutbuddin.

Kankaleshwari Kali Temple

Kankaleshwari temple is one of the famous sites in and around Barddhaman (Burdwan). There are several temples of various gods and goddesses in Burdwan but the goddess Kali is worshiped more than any other. A lot of Kali temple (Mandir) are found around Burdwan

108 Shiv Mandir

n Hindu Mythology this number 108 is considered as very holy. Accordingly this temple premises of Lord Shiva inside the town of Burdwan is comprised of 108 identical Shiva Temples. Each temple is an independent building & doesn’t have any common wall with the adjacent temple.

Chandni Park (Jaltungi)

Jaltungi is situated in the middle of a small lake at Chandni Park; Dignagar village. It was built by Maharaj Kirtichand at around 1710.
